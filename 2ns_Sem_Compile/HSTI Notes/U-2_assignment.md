**Answers (Point-wise)**

1: Historiography and its relevance to science and technology in India
- Historiography is the study of how history is written, including methods, principles, and interpretations.
- Analyzes how historians present events and select sources.
- In Indian science and technology, historiography reveals how scientific achievements are recorded and interpreted.
- Sheds light on contributions of Indian scientists and the impact of colonialism.
- Helps identify biases and gaps in historical records.
- Encourages critical evaluation of sources and narratives.

2: Colonial science and nationalist science
- Colonial science: Introduced and promoted by colonial powers, often imposed Western methods on indigenous populations.
- Focused on exploiting resources and controlling knowledge systems.
- Nationalist science: Emerged as a response, aimed to reclaim and promote indigenous scientific knowledge.
- Emphasized traditional knowledge systems and Indian contributions.
- Sought to build national pride and counter colonial narratives.
- Encouraged revival of ancient Indian scientific texts and practices.

3: Who wrote a history of Hindu Chemistry?
- P.C. Ray wrote the history of Hindu Chemistry.
- His work highlighted ancient Indian achievements in chemistry and challenged colonial views.

4: Role of the state in development of science and technology after independence
- State established institutions and research centers to promote scientific research.
- Implemented policies for collaboration between academia and industry.
- Invested in education and infrastructure for scientific development.
- Supported innovation through funding and national programs.
- Encouraged international cooperation and technology transfer.

5: Marxist interpretation of the history of science
- Emphasizes economic and social factors in shaping scientific development.
- Argues scientific progress is influenced by material conditions, class struggle, and mode of production.
- Scientific knowledge is shaped by interests of the ruling class.
- Highlights the role of labor and industry in scientific advancement.
- Critiques the notion of science as neutral or purely objective.

6: Two characteristics of nationalist interpretation of ancient Indian science
- Emphasizes achievements of ancient Indian scientists in various fields.
- Promotes pride and identity in India's scientific heritage.
- Challenges colonial narratives that downplayed Indian contributions.
- Highlights continuity and relevance of traditional knowledge.

7: Different phases of historiography of science and technology in India
- Early colonial historiography: Eurocentric, portrayed Indian science as primitive.
- Nationalist historiography: Reclaimed and promoted indigenous knowledge and achievements.
- Post-independence historiography: More nuanced, critical approach; explores interactions between indigenous and colonial traditions.
- Recent phase: Focuses on global context and interdisciplinary perspectives.

8: Two features of nationalist historiography of science and technology in India
- Emphasizes traditional knowledge systems and Indian contributions.
- Challenges colonial narratives, promotes pride in scientific heritage.
- Highlights indigenous innovation and adaptation.

9: Indigenous technology
- Refers to traditional knowledge and practices developed by indigenous communities.
- Includes agriculture, medicine, craftsmanship, and more.
- Based on deep understanding of local environments and resources.
- Provides insights into sustainable and eco-friendly practices.
- Often adapted to changing social and environmental needs.

10: Traditional knowledge systems in India
- Encompass knowledge and practices passed down through generations.
- Includes agriculture, medicine, astronomy, craftsmanship, and more.
- Rooted in local cultures and environments.
- Offer holistic approaches to health, sustainability, and well-being.
- Serve as a foundation for modern innovations and community resilience.

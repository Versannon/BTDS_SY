**Questions**

1: What is Historiography? Explain its relevance to the science and technology in india.

2: Write short note on colonial science and nationalist science.

3: Who wrote a history of Hindu Chemistry?

4: Explain the role of the state in the development of science and technology after independence.

5: Describe the marxist interpratation of the history of science.

6: State two characteristics of nationalist interpretation of ancient Indian Science.

7: Explain the different phases of historiography of science and technology in India.

8: Mention two features of nationalist historiography of scienceand technology in India.

9: Write a short note on indigenous technology.

10: Write a short on traditional knowledge system in India.

**Answers**

1: Historiography is the study of the methods and principles through which history is written. It involves analyzing how historians have interpreted and presented historical events, as well as the sources they have used. In the context of science and technology in India, historiography helps us understand how scientific developments have been recorded and interpreted over time, shedding light on the contributions of Indian scientists and the impact of colonialism on scientific progress.

2: Colonial science refers to the scientific knowledge and practices that were introduced and promoted by colonial powers in their colonies. It often involved the imposition of Western scientific methods and ideas on indigenous populations. Nationalist science, on the other hand, emerged as a response to colonial science and aimed to reclaim and promote indigenous scientific knowledge and practices. It emphasized the importance of traditional knowledge systems and sought to assert the value of Indian contributions to science.

3: The history of Hindu Chemistry was written by P.C. Ray.

4: After independence, the state played a crucial role in the development of science and technology in India. The government established various institutions and research centers to promote scientific research and innovation. It also implemented policies to encourage collaboration between academia and industry, and invested in education and infrastructure to support scientific development.

5: The Marxist interpretation of the history of science emphasizes the role of economic and social factors in shaping scientific development. It argues that scientific progress is influenced by the material conditions of society, including class struggle and the mode of production. According to this perspective, scientific knowledge is not neutral but is shaped by the interests of the ruling class.

6: Two characteristics of nationalist interpretation of ancient Indian science are:
- It emphasizes the contributions of ancient Indian scientists and their achievements in various fields of science and technology.
- It seeks to promote a sense of pride and identity in India's scientific heritage, often in response to colonial narratives that downplayed or ignored Indian contributions to science.

7: The different phases of historiography of science and technology in India can be categorized as follows:
- Early colonial historiography: This phase was characterized by a Eurocentric perspective that often portrayed Indian science as primitive and inferior to Western science.
- Nationalist historiography: This phase emerged as a response to colonial narratives and sought to reclaim
and promote indigenous scientific knowledge and achievements.
- Post-independence historiography: This phase has seen a more nuanced and critical approach to the history of science and technology in India, with an emphasis on understanding the complex interactions between indigenous and colonial scientific traditions.

8: Two features of nationalist historiography of science and technology in India are:
- It emphasizes the importance of traditional knowledge systems and seeks to assert the value of Indian contributions to science.
- It often challenges colonial narratives that downplayed or ignored Indian contributions to science, and seeks to promote a sense of pride and identity in India's scientific heritage.

9: Indigenous technology refers to the traditional knowledge and practices related to science and technology that have been developed and used by indigenous communities. This can include agricultural techniques, medicinal practices, and various forms of craftsmanship. Indigenous technology is often based on a deep understanding of local environments and resources, and can provide valuable insights into sustainable practices.

10: Traditional knowledge systems in India encompass a wide range of knowledge and practices that have been passed down through generations. This includes knowledge related to agriculture, medicine, astronomy, and various forms of craftsmanship. Traditional knowledge systems are often rooted in local cultures and environments, and can provide valuable insights into sustainable practices and holistic approaches to health and well-being.

📘 UNIT 1 – DESIGN THINKING
1. Define Design Thinking and mention its origin. (2)

Design Thinking is a human-centered problem-solving approach.

It focuses on empathy, ideation, prototyping, and testing.

Originated from design methodologies in the 1960s–70s.

Popularized by Stanford d.school and IDEO.

2. List any two reasons why Design Thinking is relevant in engineering. (2)

Encourages user-centric engineering solutions.

Promotes innovation and creativity.

Reduces risk through early testing.

Helps solve complex real-world problems.
(Any two)

3. Explain the role of empathy in Design Thinking. (2)

Helps understand user needs and emotions.

Avoids assumption-based solutions.

Identifies real pain points.

Improves solution relevance.

4. State the objective of the Define phase. (2)

To clearly frame the problem.

To analyze user insights.

To create a focused problem statement.

To guide idea generation.

5. List any two benefits of prototyping. (2)

Enables early testing of ideas.

Reduces cost of failure.

Improves design clarity.

Encourages iteration.
(Any two)

6. Explain how Design Thinking has evolved over time. (5)

Initially focused on product aesthetics.

Shifted to human-centered design.

Integrated into engineering and business.

Adopted in healthcare, finance, IT.

Now used for innovation and digital transformation.

Became a multidisciplinary framework.

7. Describe the Empathize phase with example. (5)

First stage of Design Thinking.

Understand users via interviews and observation.

Identify user needs and pain points.

Create empathy maps and personas.

Example:

Studying hospital patients to understand waiting issues.

Observations reveal long queue frustration.

8. Explain the Define phase and its importance. (5)

Analyze insights from empathy stage.

Identify core problem.

Write problem statement.

Frame “How Might We” questions.

Importance:

Prevents solving wrong problem.

Provides clarity and direction.

Improves efficiency of solutions.

9. Illustrate the Ideate phase with engineering example. (5)

Generate multiple ideas.

Use brainstorming and mind mapping.

Encourage divergent thinking.

Select feasible ideas.

Example:

Designing smart energy system:

Solar panels

AI temperature control

Smart sensors

10. Explain Prototype phase and its role. (5)

Create low-cost working model.

Can be paper, digital, or physical.

Makes ideas tangible.

Identifies design flaws.

Reduces development risk.

Encourages innovation.

11. Describe Test phase and feedback improvement. (5)

Give prototype to users.

Observe interaction.

Collect feedback.

Identify issues.

Refine solution.

Repeat process if needed.

12. Apply Design Thinking to healthcare problem. (5)

Problem: Long waiting time.

Empathize: Interview patients.

Define: Manual appointment delay.

Ideate: Online booking system.

Prototype: Simple app.

Test: Pilot in hospital.

Result: Reduced waiting time.

13. Analyze use in data-driven systems. (5)

Empathize: Understand data users.

Define: Identify analytics needs.

Ideate: Dashboard designs.

Prototype: Mock visualization.

Test: Stakeholder feedback.

Outcome: User-friendly data systems.

14. Evaluate impact in financial applications. (5)

Improves digital banking experience.

Simplifies loan processes.

Enhances fraud detection dashboards.

Increases customer trust.

Reduces operational errors.

Encourages innovation.

15. Design solution for smart city problem. (5)

Problem: Traffic congestion.

Empathize: Study commuter behavior.

Define: Poor signal timing.

Ideate: AI traffic signals.

Prototype: Smart junction.

Test: Monitor congestion.

Outcome: Reduced travel time.

📘 UNIT 2 – USER RESEARCH & DATA COLLECTION
1. Define user research and state its importance. (2)

User research is systematic study of user needs and behavior.

Helps understand user expectations.

Ensures user-centric solutions.

Reduces design failure risk.

2. List any two user research techniques. (2)

Interviews

Observation

Surveys

Contextual inquiry
(Any two)

3. Purpose of interviews in user research. (2)

Gather in-depth insights.

Understand user experiences.

Identify pain points.

Clarify user expectations.

4. Role of observation in understanding behavior. (2)

Studies real user actions.

Identifies hidden problems.

Reduces bias from self-reporting.

Captures natural behavior.

5. Define surveys and mention one advantage. (2)

Surveys are structured questionnaires used to collect user data.

Advantage: Can collect data from large number of users quickly.

6. Explain data collection in user research with example. (5)

Identify target users.

Select research method.

Collect qualitative and quantitative data.

Analyze user behavior.

Interpret insights.

Example: Collecting feedback from students about online learning platforms.

7. Describe interviews as user research technique. (5)

One-to-one conversation with users.

Can be structured or unstructured.

Provides deep qualitative insights.

Helps explore emotions and motivations.

Useful in early design stages.

8. Explain observation technique and benefits. (5)

Observe users in real environment.

Identify behavioral patterns.

Detect usability problems.

Provides real-time insights.

Reduces reliance on assumptions.

9. Illustrate journey mapping with example. (5)

Visual representation of user experience.

Shows user steps and emotions.

Identifies pain points.

Example: Mapping patient journey from appointment booking to discharge.

10. How journey mapping identifies pain points and opportunities. (5)

Highlights frustration stages.

Shows gaps in service.

Identifies improvement areas.

Helps redesign experience.

Improves customer satisfaction.

11. Describe contextual inquiry and significance. (5)

Research method involving observation + interview.

Conducted in user’s natural environment.

Provides real-world insights.

Captures contextual behavior.

Enhances solution relevance.

12. Explain how contextual inquiry supports data-driven solutions. (5)

Collects real user data.

Identifies usage patterns.

Supports evidence-based design.

Reduces design errors.

Improves system accuracy.

13. Apply user feedback to design recommendation engine. (5)

Collect user preference data.

Analyze browsing behavior.

Identify patterns.

Prototype recommendation model.

Test accuracy.

Refine using feedback.

14. Analyze role of data collection in predictive modeling. (5)

Accurate data improves predictions.

Identifies trends and patterns.

Reduces bias.

Supports machine learning models.

Improves model reliability.

15. Design data science solution using user research insights. (5)

Conduct user research.

Define problem statement.

Collect relevant dataset.

Develop predictive model.

Test with real users.

Improve using feedback.
